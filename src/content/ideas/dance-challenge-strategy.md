Here's an idea I had today that feels too good not to share:

**Student ministries could create custom tracks with Suno, launch them as TikTok dance challenges at camp, and end up with a massive library of perfectly synced footage for recap videos.**

## The Problem

Camp recap videos are hard. You're trying to:
- Capture authentic moments without making it feel staged
- Get enough footage variety to tell the story
- Make something shareable that students actually want to post
- Do all this while running the actual camp

Most teams either:
1. Scramble to get B-roll throughout the weekend
2. Hope students share their own content (which rarely happens cohesively)
3. End up with a generic montage set to a trending song

## The Concept

What if you engineered the shareable moment AND the content capture system at the same time?

Here's how it works:

### 1. Create a Custom Track

Use Suno to generate a sick, actually-bumping track that's specific to your camp. Not just any song — make it *theirs*:
- Reference the camp location
- Work in the theme
- Include inside jokes
- Make it feel like "our song" not "a song"

The production value of original music elevates everything. Plus, students aren't just dancing to someone else's trending audio — they're creating something that belongs to the ministry.

### 2. Launch as a Dance Challenge

Introduce the track at camp. Create a simple, repeatable dance. Make it the event itself.

Every student recording = another camera angle you didn't have to set up.  
Every phone = multicam coverage automatically synced to the beat.

### 3. Collect the Content

By the end of the weekend, you have:
- Dozens (maybe hundreds) of clips
- All perfectly synced to the same custom track
- Authentic energy because it wasn't "create content for us" — it was "do this fun thing"
- Multiple angles of the same moments
- Students already primed to share because they participated

### 4. The Recap Edits Itself

You've got a massive library of on-beat clips. The edit becomes a matter of selection, not scrambling for footage. And when students share their individual versions? Free promotion that doesn't feel like promotion.

## Why This Works

**It solves multiple problems at once:**
- Engagement ✓ (the challenge is the activity)
- Content capture ✓ (built into participation)
- Shareability ✓ (students want to post it)
- Camp energy ✓ (creates a memorable collective moment)

**It's operationally smart:**
You're not asking for favors ("please send me your footage"). You're designing an experience that naturally generates the content you need.

**It's emotionally resonant:**
A custom song that references their specific camp creates ownership. It becomes part of the camp's identity, not just background music.

## The Execution

1. **Pre-camp:** Generate 2-3 track options with Suno. Test them. Pick the one that slaps.
2. **Day 1 or 2:** Introduce the track. Teach the dance. Make it a thing.
3. **Throughout camp:** Let it become the soundtrack. Students will naturally start recording.
4. **Post-camp:** Collect submissions (via shared drive, DM, whatever). Edit the recap using the best moments.
5. **Share it back:** Post the official recap. Watch students repost because they're in it.

## Tools You Need

- Suno (for track generation)
- Basic video editing software (Premiere, Final Cut, CapCut, whatever)
- A shared folder or hashtag for collection
- That's it
